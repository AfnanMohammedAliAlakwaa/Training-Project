Fake News and Misinformation

“Fake news means false information that is presented as if it is real news.
Misinformation means incorrect or misleading information that is shared without checking its accuracy, even if the person sharing it does not intend to cause harm.”

Why Fake News Spreads Quickly:
1_ Social media allows instant sharing
2_ Emotional content gets more attention
3_ Algorithms promote popular posts
4_ Many users do not verify sources
5_ Influencers and celebrities can spread information rapidly

Examples and Effects of Fake News :
Common Examples :
   False health advice during pandemics
   Edited photos or videos
   Political rumors during elections
   Celebrity death hoaxes
Effects :
   Confuses the public
   Creates fear and panic
   Damages reputations
   Influences public opinion

Ethical issues :
   Dishonesty and manipulation
   Harm to society
   Loss of trust in media
   Spread of hate and division

“To reduce fake news, we should:

Always check the source of information
Compare news from reliable websites
Avoid sharing without verification
Follow trusted news platforms”
